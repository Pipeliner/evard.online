The Great Depression: Ideological Interpretations

Time Period: 1929-1939

The Great Depression was a severe worldwide economic crisis that began with the U.S. stock market crash of 1929 and lasted until the late 1930s. It led to widespread unemployment, bank failures, business closures, and a sharp decline in international trade. This unprecedented economic downturn transformed American society and government policy, sparking intense debate about its causes and appropriate responses.
